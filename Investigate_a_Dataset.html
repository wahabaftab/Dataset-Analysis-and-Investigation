Introduction¶

In this analysis we will be investigating The Movie Dataset which has the data of the movies of the last 50+ years and we'll be looking at the trends and correlation between different data features and how each generation of movies differs from one another. We'll also make our analysis for each decade where required to find better insights.

We'll be looking at the following things in our analysis:¶

Is the data clean? does it contain any zero,null or dupicate values?,

Are all the columns/rows important or are some of them redundant and can be dropped?,

How the popularity and vote_count related and what is their trend in different decades?,

What are the top cast and directors in our dataset?,

Which revenue/budget value to use (revenue/budget-adj or revenue/budget)? Which decade was most profitable?,

What are the most popular genres and keywords of each decade?,

and are all the titles in the dataset "movies"? if no then what other titles exist and how to differentiate them?

Conclusions¶

Following were the questions and our analysis of those questions:

Q1) How has the popularity and vote count increased in the last few decades.¶

The modernization of showbiz industry is causing an increase of popularity and vote_Count.The popularity and vote_Count show similar characteristics, movies are popular now than they were few decades ago and hence the vote_Count also increases.

The popularity outliers were removed based on quantile, had they been used, results might have been different. Also relating popularity and vote_count is questionable if you consider that people had almost no mediums to cast votes on movies in the earlier decades but movies were still popular, other than that this insight is pretty sound.

Q2) Which decade was the most successful monetarily?¶

Considering just revenues, the later movies made way more money as compared to the classic movies and the same goes for budget. But the classics had better revenue-budget ratio. Hence the trend shows as the time progresses budget and revenue increases but the profit ratio decreases with few exceptions.

One basic shortcoming of this insight was that we had to fill in mean values where there were zero values, as there were alot of zeros values(more than 50%).

We chose mean because it doesn't affect relationship between the variables and it is very fast. We could have used other imputations but chose mean for the sake of simplicity. These replacements are never perfect because not all missing values are equal to mean and it reduces variance in the dataset.s

Q3) What are the most popular genres and keywords of different decades.¶

Most popular genres were "Drama" and "Comedy".

Most popular keywords were "Based on novels" for earlier decades and "Women Directors" for the recent decades.

The limitation with this result is that we have based the popularity of genre and keywords purely on the number of occurences of each. There can be a lot of drama movies in a decade which have not been well received by the audience hence the popularity will be low even tho count is large.

Also keywords had many null values and they were to be removed to make keywords data usable, results would have been better if all values were non-null from the start.

Q4) Which director and actor/actress has been the most successful?¶

The most successful directors(movies more than 5 and highest mean) are "James Cameron" and "Steven Spielsberg".

The most successful Cast(movies more than 20 and highest mean) are "Will Smith" and "Tom Cruise".

Although we have collected means and counts of movies and have provided sound arguments, the threshold value can be questionable as you get different results with different threshold values and it can never be perfect.

Q5) What is the ratio of movies and tv-series in the dataset ? How to differentiate.¶

We assumed that the runtime of movies cannot exceed from a certain threshold hence why there were many outliers in the runtime, we also assumed these outliers are tv-shows (verified through manual check).We then seperated these tv-shows from movies using the threshold and found out that for 1 tv-show there are 195 movies in the dataset. And analysis of each decade showed that number of tv-shows in the later generations are increasing.

The limitation of this insight is that even though we have manually checked that shows do exist in the dataset but the threshold value is an assumption that no movie can be greater than the threshold or no show can be smaller than the threshold. It is very much possible that there are shows that have runtime less than the movies and vice versa which makes the threshold technique wrong. Also our differentiation solely depends on one value (runtime) which is not a good practice when collecting insights.

Note:¶

Although we used almost all the columns for our advantage but their were few which were untouched i.e titles, release_date, production companies etc.Had we predicted that these were not to be used in our analysis , we would have dropped them from the start. This would have made our data more compact and easier to look at.
